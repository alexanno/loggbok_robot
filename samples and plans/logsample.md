October 10 1827 Trade winds with fine weather. Fore
 
Topmast steering sail out. Steering SE.
 
Night squally. Took in sail.


11th Fine breezes pleasant weather. Nothing
 
worthy remarks. Steering SE by E.


12th Beautiful weather. Light breeze.
 
Steering SE by E. Night square the
 
yards. Head SW. Alter the course on
 
account of finding ourselves on the coast
 
of Barbary. Just ten degrees to the east ward
 
of where we ought to be owing to a mistake
 
in the Lunars.


13th Clear weather. Moderate winds. All sail set.
 
Saw Porpoises and crossed green water.
 
Lat 19=4   Long 14=16


14th The water is again coloured of a deep
 
green. Between 3 or 4PM saw a large
 
sperm whale. Lowered away 3 boats.
 
The sea running high. Was unable to
 
come up with her. Set all sail
 
again and facet on her course. Wind fair.
 
Weather cloudy. So ends.

with fine weather and moderate breezes.

irons out of the boats. At 12 oclock got
 
the whales cabled. After dinner began cut
 
in. At sundown got through cutting in &
